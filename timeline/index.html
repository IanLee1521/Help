Prior to 1996

Before USAJOBS.gov, federal hiring was a paper process. Potential applicants would have to travel to federal buildings to view job announcements on bulletin boards or posted "reading rooms".

1996 - Where It All Began

In 1996, the USAJOBS website was launched. As the single entry point for federal employment, the initial site laid the groundwork for the evolution that would take place over the next 20 years.

The original site was basic, featuring only current job openings, general information, and the ability to fill out an online application.

  • 3,000 job postings per day
  • 36,000 visits per day
  • Data was updated one time a day

1996-2001- The Early Years

Over the next 5 years, USAJOBS would evolve with the advent of technology. The website continued to enhance search, allowing users to identify jobs within a specific agency. Also, for first time, USAJOBS provided users a tool to create a resume that included their education, work experience, and other key details.
